Miami (/maɪˈæmi/), officially the City of Miami, is a coastal metropolis located in Miami-Dade County in southeastern Florida, United States. With a population of 467,963 as of the 2020 census,[7] it is the 44th-largest city in the United States and the core of the nation's eighth-largest metropolitan area.[8] The city has the third-largest skyline in the U.S. with over 300 high-rises,[12] 58 of which exceed 491 ft (150 m).[13]

Miami is a major center and leader in finance, commerce, culture, arts, and international trade.[14][15] The metro area is by far the largest urban economy in Florida and the 12th largest in the United States, with a GDP of $344.9 billion as of 2017.[16] In 2020, Miami was classified as a Beta + level global city by the GaWC.[17] In 2019, Miami ranked seventh in the United States and 31st among global cities in business activity, human capital, information exchange, cultural experience, and political engagement.[18]
